CURRENT WEATHER-HEALTH REFERENCE

High temperatures across parts of Ireland

Hot conditions can increase dehydration and heat-stress risk, especially for older adults, young children, pregnant people, outdoor workers and people with existing health conditions.

  • Drink water regularly rather than waiting until you feel thirsty.
  • Choose hydrating foods such as fruit, vegetables, yoghurt and light meals.
  • Avoid prolonged outdoor activity during the hottest part of the day.
  • Use shade, light clothing and sun protection, and check on vulnerable people.
  • Seek urgent help for confusion, collapse, very high temperature or loss of consciousness.
