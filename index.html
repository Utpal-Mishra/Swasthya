CURRENT WEATHER-HEALTH REFERENCE

High temperatures across parts of Ireland

Hot conditions can increase dehydration and heat-stress risk, especially for vulnerable people.

  • Drink water regularly and choose hydrating foods.
  • Avoid prolonged outdoor activity during the hottest part of the day.
  • Use shade, light clothing and sun protection.
  • Seek urgent help for confusion, collapse or loss of consciousness.

WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

Decision support, not just another map or forecast

Each alert should help the user decide what to do next, explain how certain the signal is and avoid unnecessary notification noise.

Action first

One clear next step

Lead with the most useful action—hydrate, avoid peak heat, check air quality or seek urgent support—before showing secondary detail.

Freshness

Show when data changed

Display observation time, retrieval time and stale-data status so yesterday’s conditions are never presented as current.

Confidence

Explain uncertainty

Separate official observations, modelled estimates and manually maintained references rather than implying equal reliability.

Route aware

Offer safer alternatives

Future routing should consider heat, air quality, shade, distance and opening hours—not only the shortest route.

Low clutter

Keep the map readable

Use progressive disclosure: show the essential alert first and let users expand sources, thresholds and methodology when needed.

Alert control

Reduce notification fatigue

Notify only for new or materially changed high-priority events, with user-selected categories, severity and quiet periods.
